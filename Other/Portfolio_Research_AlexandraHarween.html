Here are the websites I consulted when researching porftolio sites:

  • Codementor.io - How to Make a Killer Software Engineer Resume
  • Ultimate Guide to Creating a Programmer Portfolio
  • Build a Software Developer Portfolio Website in 5 Days
  • Explore Minimalist Web Design(this was for style inspiration)
I learned the following from reading through the articles above.

The "Ultimate Guide" article summarizes 3 key areas to cover: About Me, Projects, and Contact.

Obviously, potential employers want to know what skills you have. Include these both in written format such as a list of languages and certifications and also show examples. Take screenshots of your projects or your websites and write a brief description of the project, its challenges, and any problems you solved.

The design, set-up and coding of your portfolio themselves will speak to your knowledge and work. Is it well-organized? Clear? Pleasing to look at? Functional? If you are a designer, more weight is placed on the aesthetics, but even for backend developers you need to have a nice-looking site.

Soft Skills: All of the articles said that these are important. It's important that you can collaborate, problem-solve, and manage your time. Do you have leadership skills? It's also OK to let some of your personality show through, whether it's from your writing style, a photograph, or a description of what you're like.
